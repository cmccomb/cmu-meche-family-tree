This report summarizes the academic lineage reconstructed from primary and secondary historical sources. The evidence is strongest for the links from Stryk to Decher and from Decher to Wolff. At present, no reliable source identifies Wolff's own doctoral advisor.

## Notes

Stryk studied at Wittenberg under Caspar Ziegler and Wilhelm Leyser II before returning to Frankfurt (Oder), where he earned his law degrees in 1665 and later became a leading jurist of the usus modernus pandectarum.

Decher's funeral-sermon biography records philosophy studies at Wittenberg, a Magister degree in 1636, later legal studies at Frankfurt (Oder), and the doctorate awarded on 17 June 1647. Because Wolff presided over the doctoral act, he is treated here as the appropriate doctoral-advisor equivalent for this period.

Wolff is documented as a jurist and professor at Frankfurt (Oder), including Professor of the Codex Justinianus and the Pandects, rector, and dean of the law faculty. Sources consistently document his career after becoming professor, but not the professor under whom he received his own doctorate.

Recovering Wolff's own advisor would likely require examining unpublished or partially digitized faculty promotion registers, doctoral act books, or seventeenth-century Viadrina archival records.
